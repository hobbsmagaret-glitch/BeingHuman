October 21, 2003 — Denver, Colorado
The Accident That Changed Everything

Brandon crashed a motorcycle at over 100 miles an hour. No helmet. He hit a tree, bounced off a rod-iron fence lining a cemetery, and shattered — breaking his arm, leg, and snapping his pelvis in half.

More critically: he severed his femoral artery — one of the largest in the human body — and suffered a severe traumatic brain injury. The medical staff at Denver Health told his family it was a very big “if” he would survive.

Every department chief was pulled into an emergency meeting. That meeting was interrupted to have everyone scrub in for surgery. It took nearly every department head to save his life.

He survived. And what he discovered rebuilding himself became the foundation of everything he now teaches.

You Are a Perfect Machine
Rebuilding from TBI, fractured bones and a severed femoral artery forced Brandon into a first-principles understanding of the human body. That revelation became the Being Human Handbook and the Midas Touch Movement.
